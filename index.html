Mapping New York City’s Environmental Impact on Pediatric Asthma

The repository for this project is here.

Group members: Shayne Estill(ske2118), Woojin Shin(ws2721), Naomi Simon-Kumar(ns3782), Serena Ting(sjt2164)

Our project seeks to analyze how environmental factors shape children’s respiratory health in New York City during a time of increasing climate change. As urban environments face rising temperatures, changing air quality patterns, and pressures on existing greenspace, understanding these environmental impacts on pediatric asthma is critical to addressing the burden of asthma in New York City, particularly in communities facing health inequities.

Using linear modeling and other statistical methods, we analyze data across two time periods (2016-2018 and 2019-2021) to investigate the relationships between emergency department visits, air quality (PM2.5), temperature patterns, and greenspace distribution, examining how these climate-sensitive factors affect children’s health outcomes across different areas of New York City.

Main Research Questions

  • What is the relationship between environmental factors (PM2.5, temperature, greenspace, Heat Vulnerability Index (HVI)) and pediatric asthma emergencies?
  • Which environmental factors show the strongest associations with pediatric asthma emergencies?
  • How are these environmental health burdens geographically distributed across New York City?
  • Is HVI a good model predictor for pediatric asthma emergencies?

We believe that understanding these relationships is crucial for informing equitable, climate-resilient urban planning policies and public health initiatives aimed at protecting and improving children’s health in New York City, especially in communities most vulnerable to environmental stressors.
